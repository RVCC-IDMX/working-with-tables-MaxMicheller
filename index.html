Most Devistating Earthquakes by Max Micheller

Location; Date Magnitude Depth of Epicenter Fatalities Damage
Shaanxi, China; January 23, 1556 8.0 Unknown 820,000-830,000 Depth and Distruction affecting as far as 500 kilometres from the epicenter.
Hebei, China; July 28, 1976 7.8 39.63°N 118.10°E 39.72°N 118.44°E A: 12.2 km B: 16.7 km In just minutes, 85% of buildings collapsed, and all services failed.
Ningxia–Gansu, China; December 16, 1920 7.8 36.50°N 105.70°E 273,400 A landslide buried the village of Sujiahe, damage occurred in seven provinces and regions.
Antioch, Byzantine Empire; May 21, 526 7.0 36.23°N 36.12°E 250,000 Many buildings damaged, most damage was from the resulting fires that went on for many days after the earthquake.
Sumatra, Indonesia; December 26, 2004 9.1-9.3 30 km (19 mi) 227,898 Most damage was done by the resulting tsunami, which swept away most buldings except for some of the already damaged concrete buildings.
Ganja, Azerbaijan; September 30, 1139 7.0 23 km 230,000–300,000 Many buildings totally destroyed (not much info)
Aleppo, Syria; October 11, 1138 7.1 36°13′N 37°10′E 130,000–230,000 This earthquake was followed by another shortly after.
Haiti; January 12, 2010 7.0 13 km (8.1 mi) 100,000–316,000 250,000 residencies and 30,000 commercial buildings had collapsed or were severly damaged.
Shanxi, China; July 25, 1303 7.2-7.6 200,000 36.3°N 111.7°E nearly 100,000 hourses collapsed and over 200,000 people died from collapsing buildings.
